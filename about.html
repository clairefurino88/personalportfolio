Opinions on...

Deep Learning

Don't get me wrong, deep learning is moving technology in the right direction. But as one of my mentors said to me before, deep learning has a fundamental flaw. It is not that it does not work - it is that we do not know why exactly it works. In deep learning, empiricism rules, unlike in math or physics. It is grounded in concept and theory, but our knowledge of its mechanics is derived from practice. A computer does not learn like a human - we do not explain what a circle looks like to a computer as we would to a human. And what's scarier is that predictions are deterministic. We don't understand why that self-driving car hit someone... and it would do so every time.

Human Destiny

My opinion on this is partially inspired by Elon Musk. I believe that humans are destined to escape the confines of our planet (and maybe solar system) and colonize other planets in the galaxy. We are curious creatures by nature and have ceaselessly discovered more and more about who we are and the universe that we live in. I think that the universe is ours to grasp and understand, we just have to do our physics homework to get there.

A Next Boom

Our world is dominated by tech giants whose influences have extended across multiple industries. One particular industry - social networking/services - has been a source of success as the introduction of the World Wide Web pushed many platforms of communication onto the Internet. The frontier is shifting, however and there will inevitably be another spike with the improvement of space travel and AI - and possibly a monumental shift in how we know technology.
