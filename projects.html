My Projects

UnitedHealth Group

  • Reading Comprehension

    I have worked with reading comprehension to fit a huge use case: reducing employee lookup time in documentation by adapting a reading comprehension model - Google's QANet - to healthcare purposes so that answering to patient questions in real-time can be automated.

    I have improved near state-of-the-art inference accuracy by 4% and seek to continue to improve performance through data augmentation and hyperparameter tuning.

  • Topic Analysis

    Given the abundance of patient recordings and transcriptions from surveys, my task was to perform topic analysis on them and generate clusters of topics. I designed and implemented a complete pipeline - word and sentence embeddings, clusterings, and projections for visualization - that generates well-defined clusters by topic and is reusable for future datasets.

    There are aspects of deep learning with the usage of ELMo for word embeddings as well as traditional NLP methods such as k-Means to generate the clusters and t-SNE to produce 3D vectors for visualization.

Personal Projects

ThoughtCloud

ThoughtCloud is an extension of my vision of accessible creativity. It is only by contemplating the nature of our existence - our world and society - that we will be able to perpetuate technological advancement. My co-developer, Eric Gan, and I hope to facilitate the dissemination of concepts and ideas through this platform by scraping together general ideas, filtered by topic, and providing that as a real-time refreshable feed for each user.

ThoughtCloud is currently locally-run on Flask and scrapes results from pre-selected websites, allowing for filter by search keywords. We hope to expand this service in the future by dropping the use of a scraper altogether and implement it as more of a search engine.

uHealth

uHealth is a prototype demo created by Team Ingenuity at Optum's Boston Hackathon. Perhaps the biggest problem in healthcare is efficient allocation of resources, which also includes patient adherence to the healthcare plan provided. uHealth hopes to solve this by providing easy access to an individual's data (i.e. real-time figures, appointments, prescriptions, history) as well as an analytics tab that makes predictions.

uHealth is locally-run on npm and the analytics tab features a diabetic's prescription refills as predicted by RandomForest Regression, which is trained on Kaggle datasets.
